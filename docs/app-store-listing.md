# App Store Listing — StayConnected

Draft marketing copy for submission. Positioning is grounded in research on
why people abandon keep-in-touch apps: guilt framing and manual logging drive
uninstalls, while "helpful without being naggy" and on-device privacy win.

## App name
StayConnected — Reach out gently

## Subtitle (30 chars)
Stay close, without the guilt

## Promotional text (170 chars)
A calm daily nudge to reach out to the people who matter. No streaks to shame
you, no logging chores — tap to call and it just remembers. Private, on your
device.

## Keywords (100 chars)
keep in touch,stay in touch,friends,family,reconnect,relationships,reminder,call,contacts,cadence

## Description

Life gets busy and the people we care about quietly drift. StayConnected is a
gentle way to keep those relationships warm — a small daily nudge, never a
guilt trip.

Each day it suggests a few people to reach out to, chosen by how overdue they
are for *your* rhythm with them. Tap to call or message and the check-in logs
itself — no "did you remember to mark it?" busywork. Too busy today? Snooze
anyone with a gentle "not today" and we'll suggest someone else.

Why StayConnected feels different:

• A few people a day, not an endless to-do list
• Set a rhythm per person — close, regular, or occasional
• Reaching out is one tap, and it's remembered for you
• Jot a one-line note ("ask about her new job") for next time
• A gentle daily reminder that adapts to your day
• No pace-shaming, no red "you're behind" numbers

Private by design:
• Everything stays on your device
• No account and no sign-up
• Contacts access only when you choose to add someone

StayConnected isn't a productivity tool or a CRM. It's a quiet companion that
helps you be a little more present with the people who matter.

## What's New (first release notes)
The first release of StayConnected: gentle daily picks, per-person cadence,
one-tap reach-out that logs itself, "not today" snooze, and quick context
notes — all private, on your device.

## Privacy nutrition (App Privacy answers)
- Data collected: None.
- Data linked to you: None.
- Tracking: None.
- Notes: All data (pool, history, notes) is stored locally in Core Data on the
  device. Contacts are read only to add people the user selects; contact data
  is not uploaded anywhere.

## Positioning notes (internal)
- The recommended keep-in-touch app for years, Garden, is now abandoned and
  unstable — there is a searching, under-served audience.
- Lead with "gentle / private / no account / helpful without being naggy,"
  which is exactly what winning competitor reviews praise.
- Avoid CRM language ("manage your network") — the research shows that framing
  is what makes people bounce.
